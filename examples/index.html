Examples Elsewhere

Successful models from other cities that have implemented innovative approaches to improving food and pharmacy access in underserved communities. These examples provide a roadmap for Seattle's hub-and-spoke grocery-pharmacy network.

Seattle's Urgent Need

Recent closures of Bartell Drugs (acquired by Rite Aid), Kroger stores, and other pharmacy chains have created significant access gaps across Seattle neighborhoods. This pilot addresses the immediate need for combined grocery-pharmacy services in underserved areas.

Urban Models

Minneapolis - Healthy Corner Store Initiative

A city-wide program that transformed corner stores into healthy food access points through grants, technical assistance, and community partnerships. This model directly informs Seattle's grocery-pharmacy corner store approach.

Key Results:

  • • 150+ stores participating in healthy food program
  • • 35% increase in fresh produce sales
  • • $2.3M in economic impact for participating stores
  • • Improved health outcomes in target neighborhoods

Seattle Adaptation:

  • • Combine grocery transformation with pharmacy services
  • • Leverage former Bartell/Rite Aid locations as hubs
  • • Partner with local corner stores for satellite locations
  • • Integrate telepharmacy consultations

Philadelphia - Fresh Food Financing Initiative

A public-private partnership that provided financing and technical support to develop grocery stores in underserved areas. This financing model can support Seattle's hub-and-spoke network development.

Key Results:

  • • 88 new grocery stores opened
  • • 5,000+ jobs created
  • • $78M in private investment leveraged
  • • 400,000+ residents gained access to fresh food

Seattle Application:

  • • Repurpose closed pharmacy locations as financing targets
  • • Create public-private partnerships for corner store upgrades
  • • Leverage city economic development funds
  • • Partner with healthcare systems for pharmacy integration

Rural Models

Vermont - Farm-to-Pharmacy Program

An innovative model that combines local food distribution with pharmacy services in rural communities.

Key Results:

  • • 25 rural pharmacies participating
  • • 60% of participants report improved diet quality
  • • $500K in local food sales
  • • Reduced transportation barriers for seniors

North Carolina - Mobile Market Program

A mobile grocery store that brings fresh food directly to underserved rural communities on a regular schedule.

Key Results:

  • • 12 rural communities served
  • • 2,500+ families reached monthly
  • • 40% reduction in food insecurity
  • • $1.2M in local economic impact

International Examples

London - Healthy Start Program

A comprehensive approach combining pharmacy services with nutrition support and community health initiatives.

Key Results:

  • • 200+ pharmacies providing nutrition counseling
  • • 30% improvement in maternal health outcomes
  • • £5M in healthcare cost savings
  • • Strong community engagement and trust

Toronto - Community Health Centers

Integrated health centers that combine medical care, pharmacy services, and food access programs under one roof.

Key Results:

  • • 15 integrated health centers
  • • 50,000+ residents served annually
  • • 25% reduction in emergency room visits
  • • Improved medication adherence rates

Lessons for Seattle

Success Factors

  • • Strong public-private partnerships
  • • Community engagement from planning stage
  • • Flexible funding mechanisms
  • • Data-driven site selection
  • • Comprehensive support services

Challenges to Address

  • • Initial capital investment requirements
  • • Staff recruitment and retention
  • • Supply chain logistics
  • • Regulatory compliance
  • • Long-term sustainability planning
